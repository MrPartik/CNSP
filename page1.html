Welcome Message

 The Child Neurology Society Philippines and the Philippine League Against Epilepsy, welcome all the delegates to the Joint Child Neurology Society Philippines - Philippine League Against Epilepsy Symposium on Status Epilepticus & 9th PLAE National Epilepsy Congress.

The choice of theme - Status Epilepticus - is timely as the challenges in management and associated significant morbidity and mortality are still compelling.

Back-to-back with the CNSP - PLAE Symposium on Status Epilepticus is the 2-day National Epilepsy Congress of the Philippine League Against Epilepsy . The scientific program contains a wealth of highly relevant issues in epilepsy.

On the last day, the Asian Epilepsy Academy will run a whole day course on Video-EEG. This will further hone the skills of the participants in the technical and clinical aspects.

While we assure the participants of a highly intellectual feast, we encourage everyone to enjoy Cebu - its gastronomic feast, sights and sounds and the warm smiles of the Filipinos.

Welcome everyone!

        Status epilepticus is one of the most common neurologic emergencies particularly in the pediatric age group. Through the years, new concepts have evolved with regards to its definition, types, pathophysiology, manifestations and management. The scientific committee of this symposium has prepared a comprehensive program on status epilepticus in infants and children consisting of 2 plenary lectures, 10 symposium lectures, platform research presentations as well as poster sessions. A panel discussion on how status epilepticus is managed in certain countries in the region will likewise cap the meeting.

The PLAE National Epilepsy Congress highlights key issues in epilepsy diagnosis and management including the latest ILAE Classification of seizures and epilepsies, neurobiology of anti-epileptic drugs, and adolescent issues in epilepsy. Further, a whole day course on technical and clinical aspects of Video-EEG will be offered on the last day.

On behalf of the other members of the scientific committees, may we extend our sincere appreciation to our esteemed speakers who accepted our invitation to share with us their expertise in the field of epilepsy. We also welcome our delegates and we hope that this meeting will lead to better understanding and management of status epilepticus and the epilepsies.
